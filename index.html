This is the story of Medusa

Medusa, the daughter of Phorcys and Ceto, the primordial sea Gods of Greek Mythology was one of three sisters born of the island of Sarpedon. Unlike her sisters, Medusa was mortal and thus susceptible to the effects of nature and the whims of the gods. She was enchanting and greatly desired by others. Despite her great beauty, Medua pledges herself to the goddess Athena. She takes the vows of celibacy that were required of Athena's priestesses. Life takes a sharp turn for Medusa when Poseidon, the god of the seas laid his eyes on Medusa.
